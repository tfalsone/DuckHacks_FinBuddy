The 50/20/30 Rule

When creating a budget, it is good practice to combine expenses to follow what is known as the 50/20/30 rule. The benifit of the rule is that it groups expenses together, making them easier to track.



The 50/20/30 rule is broken down below:

1. Fixed costs that stay the same each month should take up 50% of your income, and are categorized as living expenses.

2. Variable costs that change month to month should take up 30% of your income, and are categorized as food expenses.

3. Savings should take up 20% of your income.
